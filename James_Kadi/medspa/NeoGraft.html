NeoGraft


What to Expect During Your NeoGraft Procedure

Prior to your procedure, we will map out the recipient sites with an eye for pleasing and natural-looking placement, paying particular attention to the orientation, angle, and direction of the follicles. Both the donor and recipient sites will be numbed with a local anesthetic. A mild oral sedative is sometimes prescribed to help you stay relaxed. Deeper sedation is also available to some patients under certain circumstances, but it is generally not necessary. Dr. Kadi and his team of expert nurses and technicians will begin by meticulously harvesting hair follicles from your donor site using NeoGraft’s automated technology.

After the appropriate amount of follicular units have been gently extracted, they are individually transplanted to the mapped out areas, as determined during your consultation and pre-procedure planning. After the procedure, most of our patients are able to resume their normal daily activities fairly quickly. Most return to work in as little as 1 to 2 weeks. Because NeoGraft does not require the incision and staples of other surgical hair restoration methods, you will experience minimal downtime and little-to-no discomfort in the days after treatment.

When You’ll See Results

The final step in your hair transplantation journey with NeoGraft is patience. Hair transplantation is such a successful and natural-looking hair restoration option because it uses your hair follicles—which means you should anticipate seeing your results appear gradually. Follicles are living tissues that grow in a cycle, so you’ll see new growth emerging over the course of a few months.



NeoGraft results take shape over just a few months.* Within two to three weeks of your procedure, your grafted hair will shed so new growth can occur. You should begin seeing this new growth by around the three month mark, with your results gradually improving over time. Once the hair has come in and you note a fuller, healthier head of hair, you can cut, dye, and style it as you normally would.
While the transplanted hair will continue to grow, it’s important to remember that hair loss will not cease after transplantation. Over time, you may notice new areas of thinning or the beginnings of different bald spots. With this in mind, understand that you may require ongoing maintenance or additional NeoGraft treatments as time goes by.
